## Sequence Timings

### Warm Lead Sequence (Score 80+)

**DAY 1 - 9:00 AM:**
- Send Email (Personalized by farm size)
- Email angle: Compliance urgency + direct offer
- Call to action: Schedule 15-min call

**DAY 2 - 2:00 PM:**
- Send LinkedIn Connection request (if not connected)
- Message: Simple reference to email

**DAY 3 - 10:00 AM:**
- Check: Did they open email? Did they reply?
- If opened: Send follow-up email variant
- If not opened: Send LinkedIn message reminder
- Message: "Saw you in our network, worth connecting on this"

**DAY 5 - 3:00 PM:**
- If no reply: Send LinkedIn message (different angle)
- Message: "Got a minute for a quick question?"
- Goal: Get at least ONE response

**DAY 7 - 4:00 PM:**
- If interested: You take it (warm lead ready)
- If no response: Move to medium nurture (weekly tips)
- Track: Why didn't they respond? Bad timing? Wrong angle?

---

### Medium Lead Sequence (Score 50-80)

**DAY 1 - 9:00 AM:**
- Send Email (Cost savings or time savings angle)
- Less urgent than warm
- Call to action: "Reply with interest level"

**DAY 3 - 2:00 PM:**
- Check: Did they open?
- If opened but not replied: Send follow-up email
- If not opened: Send LinkedIn message
- Message: "Following up on email"

**DAY 7 - 10:00 AM:**
- If interested: You take it
- If no response: Move to nurture
- Nurture sequence: Weekly tips (Tuesday emails)
- Goal: Keep them warm for future

**WEEKLY (Ongoing):**
- Tuesday 9:00 AM: Send tip email (no sales pitch)
  - "3 ways to reduce manure disposal costs"
  - "New RVO guidelines explained"
  - "Q&A: Common compliance questions"
  - Light mention of Mestloket at bottom
- Keep touching base until they're ready

---

### Cold Lead Sequence (Score <50)

**DAY 1-30: NO ACTIVE OUTREACH**
- Reason: Low score = low priority
- Cost: Wasted effort on unlikely converts
- Strategy: Wait for engagement signals

**IF ENGAGEMENT SIGNAL RECEIVED:**
- They viewed LinkedIn profile?
- They opened marketing email from someone else?
- They attended industry event?
- Update score + move to medium sequence

**WEEKLY NURTURE (Passive):**
- Add to content list (monthly newsletter)
- No direct outreach
- Keep awareness up (low cost)
- Goal: Convert over time if circumstances change

## A/B Testing Variants

### Message Angle A/B Tests (Week 1-2)

**TEST 1: ANGLE COMPARISON**

**Variant A: "Compliance Urgency"**
- Focus: 2026 deadline, fines, risk
- Tone: Urgent, professional
- CTA: "Schedule compliance call"
- Target: Large farms
- Hypothesis: High urgency = high response

**Variant B: "Cost Savings"**
- Focus: Money saved, ROI, budget efficiency
- Tone: Friendly, practical
- CTA: "See your savings"
- Target: Medium farms
- Hypothesis: Financial incentive = high response

**Variant C: "Time Savings"**
- Focus: Automation, efficiency, convenience
- Tone: Casual, helpful
- CTA: "Try automation free"
- Target: Small farms
- Hypothesis: Time value = high response

**ASSIGNMENT:**
- Week 1: 50 farmers per variant (150 total)
- Metrics: Open rate, reply rate, quality of reply
- Winner: Highest reply + quality
- Week 2: 60% winner, 30% control, 10% new test

**EXPECTED WINNER:**
- Large farms: Compliance angle (urgency)
- Medium farms: Cost savings angle (money)
- Small farms: Time savings angle (convenience)

---

### Subject Line A/B Tests (Week 2-3)

**TEST 2: SUBJECT LINE OPTIMIZATION**

**For Large Farms:**
- Variant A: "2026 compliance deadline: Here's what you need"
- Variant B: "Avoid €50,000+ fines (compliance toolkit inside)"
- Variant C: "Your manure surplus: Liability or asset?"
- Metric: Email open rate (target: >25%)

**For Medium Farms:**
- Variant A: "Save €500/month on manure disposal"
- Variant B: "One medium farm earned €6K/year from surplus"
- Variant C: "Quick question: How much do you spend on disposal?"
- Metric: Email open rate (target: >22%)

**For Small Farms:**
- Variant A: "Stop spending 10 hours/year on manure records"
- Variant B: "Manure compliance automation (15 min setup)"
- Variant C: "50 Dutch farms automated their compliance (you can too)"
- Metric: Email open rate (target: >18%)

**ASSIGNMENT:**
- Week 2: 30 farmers per variant per segment (270 total)
- Metrics: Open rate, click rate
- Winner: Highest open + click
- Week 3: Roll out winning subject lines to all new farmers

---

### Channel Timing A/B Tests (Week 3-4)

**TEST 3: CHANNEL SEQUENCING**

**Variant A: Email First → LinkedIn**
- Day 1 9am: Send email
- Day 2 2pm: Send LinkedIn message
- Day 5 3pm: Send LinkedIn reminder
- Hypothesis: Email opens, LinkedIn completes

**Variant B: LinkedIn First → Email**
- Day 1 9am: Send LinkedIn connection
- Day 1 2pm: Send LinkedIn message
- Day 2 10am: Send email
- Hypothesis: LinkedIn breaks ice, email closes

**Variant C: Email Only**
- Day 1 9am: Send email
- Day 5 10am: Send email follow-up
- Day 10: Move to nurture
- Hypothesis: Email is enough, no LinkedIn needed

**ASSIGNMENT:**
- Week 3: 50 farmers per variant (150 total)
- Metrics: First response time, response rate, quality
- Winner: Fastest + highest quality response
- Week 4: Roll out winning sequence to all new contacts
